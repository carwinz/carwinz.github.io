Zimmermann family history
10 Dec 2019 · Carwin

Ernst and Wilhelmina Zimmermann brought the Zimmermann family from Germany to Australia.

See below for the timeline and a historical context.

Timeline

  • 13 May 1841
    Ernst was born in Wittenhof, Prenzlau, Uckermark, Brandenburg, Prussia

  • 30 January 1845
    Wilhelmine Christine Schulz was born in Bristol near Menkin, Brandenburg, Prussia
  • date unknown
    Ernst and Wilhelmina were married
  • 21 Mar 1871
    Wilhelmine and Ernst had a daughter who they named Anna Ernestine Ulrike Zimmermann.

  • 1 December 1871

    They boarded the "Herschel" in Hamburg and took a four month voyage to Australia, landing in Moreton Bay on 7 March 1872. The boat carried 250 immigrants including Wilhelmine's sisters Frederick and Ernestina (her brother and parents also moved to Australia in 1873)

  • 1872 - 1893
    The family lived in Lowood. Ernst had several occupations during this time.

  • 1872 - 1893
    1893 the Zimmermann family moved to Englesburg in the Fassifern

  • 1916

    Englesburg was renamed to Kalbar due to anti-German sentiment during World War I1.

    Interestingly, the name of the British Royal Family was changed from the German Saxe-Coburg-Gotha to the English Windsor in 1917, also due to anti-German sentiment.

Historical context

In the late middle ages, Germany was not a united country like it is today. Simplisticly speaking, it was a collection of smaller kingdoms or duchies, with an emperor elected to look out for the interests of the whole.

  • 1618 to 1648

    The Thirty years war involved most of the European powers. Because of its central position, Germany suffered greatly during the war; a third of the population perished. Perhaps up to 1800 state-lets came into being in the aftermath.

  • 1701

    The Kingdom of Prussia was formed after the union of Brandenburg and the Duchy of Prussia.

  • 1756 to 1763

    Seven Years' War involved all the major powers in Europe. Austria and Prussia had their own conflict as the both sought to gain control of Germany. Prussia survived against a collition of France, Austria and Russia thanks to Britian pumping money into Prussia.

  • 1806

    Napoleon's French army conquered Germany and formed the Confederation of the Rhine to provide him with resources and a buffer from Prussia and Austria. He then defeated the Prussians and made it subordinate to Russia

    In 1813, the Confederation of the Rhine collapsed after Napolean's loss in Russia.

  • 1848

    Harvests failed and there was a massive revolution in Germany

  • 1914-1918

    World War I ends the Prussia Empire

  • 1939-1945

    World War II started with much of the discontent and radical ideas coming from what used to be Prussia
